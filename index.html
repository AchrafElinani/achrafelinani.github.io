About

Background

Electrical and Computer Engineering major at The Ohio State University, with a double minor in Economics and English. My technical focus spans semiconductor design, verification, and manufacturing.

Research focus

I study how legal frameworks and economic incentives shape semiconductor investment, supply chain structure, and domestic manufacturing policy, with particular attention to U.S. competitiveness in advanced chip production.

Approach

Understanding the economic and legal landscape surrounding semiconductors shapes how I approach engineering problems. Building things well requires knowing why they matter and where they fit in a larger system.

Currently

Preparing to join TSMC Arizona in support of Fab 21, a $165 billion facility central to the United States' effort to reshore advanced semiconductor manufacturing for AI and next-generation computing.

Academic Papers & Publications

Industrial Policy, Semiconductor Exports, and Economic Growth: The Case of Taiwan
The Ohio State University, Department of Economics
Analyzing Taiwan's strategic concentration in semiconductor manufacturing and how it produced sustained trade surpluses, attracted foreign capital, and generated the macroeconomic conditions for one of the most rapid economic transformations of the modern era.
Macroeconomics Open Economy Industrial Strategy Taiwan Trade Policy
In Progress
Subsidizing Sovereignty: The Legal Architecture of the CHIPS and Science Act
The Undergraduate Law Review at Ohio State
An examination of the CHIPS and Science Act's statutory framework, analyzing how its subsidy mechanisms, regulatory conditions, and enforcement provisions function as instruments of industrial policy and whether its legal architecture is sufficient to achieve long-term semiconductor sovereignty.
